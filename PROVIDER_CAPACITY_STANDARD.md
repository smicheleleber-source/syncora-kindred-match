# Provider Capacity Standard

## Purpose

Measure provider capacity, responsiveness, and ability to accept new opportunities.

Capacity should influence matching recommendations.

## Availability Status

* Accepting New Clients
* Limited Availability
* Waitlist
* Not Accepting New Clients

## Capacity Indicators

### Team Size

* Solo Provider
* Small Team (2–5)
* Growing Team (6–15)
* Established Team (16–50)
* Large Organization (50+)

### Team Composition

* Professional Staff Count
* Support Staff Count
* Volunteer Count
* Administrative Staff Count

### Service Capacity

Examples:

* Active Cases
* Available Client Slots
* Available Volunteer Positions
* Available Event Capacity

### Geographic Coverage

* Local
* Regional
* Statewide
* National
* Virtual

### Response Expectations

* Same Day
* 24 Hours
* 48 Hours
* 1 Week

## Matching Considerations

High urgency requests may prioritize:

* Accepting New Clients
* Larger Capacity
* Faster Response Times

Relationship-focused requests may prioritize:

* Solo Providers
* Boutique Firms
* Specialized Organizations

## MVP Rule

Capacity information should be visible to users but should not automatically exclude providers from matching results.
